About Myself

As a kid I spend my time tinkering with electronics, doing chores I didn't like and playing... off course {{ ":wink:" | emojify }}. I taught myself how to program computers through online tutorials and books that my pops had lying around the house. Apparently during his economics studies in the 80's, they also received some programming courses in C and C++ because I found those books in his collection. The internet tutorials I would download from the internet when my parents weren't home. I would quickly dial-in to the web and download whatever I could process in a week and quickly log off. Pops and moms never caught me in action {{ ":smile:" | emojify }}.

My father would always remind me not to wreck the computer when I was still using his', because it also served as his work machine, which prevented me from going crazy on the box. After buying my father's old computer with some hard-earned cash I started hacking without limitations. By the time I finished high-school I had to choose what to study. Computer sciences did seem interesting, but I figured I could learn that from books and brain-picking. Electronics, on the other hand, often required access to some tools and equipment I couldn't get my hands on because of the financial aspect. That is why I studied electronics. I wanted to be able to program computers, build robots and rule my universe in the way I deemed appropriate.

I did study electronics. T'was a wonderful time. After graduating I set foot in the real world by beginning my free-lancing adventures. I have built plenty of cool products, made stupid mistakes (lessons), discovered more of my strengths and weaknesses and am learning truck-loads of new things through it all. Most importantly, however; I am having a blast! On sunny {{ ":sunny:" | emojify }} and rainy {{ ":umbrella:" | emojify }} days.
