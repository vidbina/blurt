--- layout: post title: Tear Down the Walls categories: [] tags: [] status: draft type: post published: false meta: blogger_blog: kingvidbina.blogspot.com blogger_author: King Vid Bina blogger_8dd67d9df101cf894e37b1fb706600b1_permalink: '3875432712856957629' --- Take a quick cruise through the world of the internet - Facebook, Twitter, MySpace and whatnot. All these tools are somewhat allowing us to tear down the apparent walls of distance that exist between us, humans.

A few years ago it would be near impossible to write to a big producer and receive as much as a reply stating "I've received your message". Nowadays, being able to communicate with such figures is almost just as common as brushing teeth (hope that brushing is common to you too). I've tried hooking up with a few established producers and musicians asking anything ranging from technical advice to personal experience, and many of them find the time to actually reply.

The mobility that this medium boasts is probably one of the reasons why this is possible. I can check tweets while standing in line at the grocery store or travelling by train (after having read the newspapers that another passenger left on the seat first).

Another big thing on this "wow, people actually reply to stuff I ask"
