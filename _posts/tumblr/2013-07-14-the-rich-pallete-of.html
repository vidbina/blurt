The Rich Pallete of…

I just arrived home after my first encounter with wine tasting ever. A couple of great and mediocre flavors (personal opinion) with a aromatic palette ranging from fermenting mangos, to bananas and wooden barrels. The most intriguing palette however was the stunning palette of qualities from people from all walks of live that I encountered.

I’m quite aware of the fact that humans are utterly fascinating creatures. I’m quite aware of the fact that every encounter is a dormant suprise waiting to errupt in a glorious cacophony of glorious discoveries, yet I still manage to get caught off guard.

Good wines and wonderful conversations. From Roy the philosopher who has mentioned some interesting facts about the works of Plato, Aristotles and Anaximander (whom I had never heard of before) to Jacqueline, a vibrant woman who managed to unload a motherload of positive energy (without attempting to sound mystical at all, she was just the type of person you would love to meet again and again). I haven’t yet mentioned anything about this amazing Rob character, but that is a story for another time.

Some people are wonderful and I’m convinced that expression of such observations is something we could do more often. Take a good look at the next remarkable specimen you encounter and express your gratitude for their acquaintance.

But yes… where were we? A wine tasting. I would recommend Dona Dolça Moscatel. A dessert wine with a refreshingly fresh (is there any other fresh?) aroma. To me the aromatic freshness nearly approached the level of “medically sterile”. In retrospect it is probably a wrong choice of words for someone who’s trying to get readers to try the wine in question but this was my first impression. It’s pretty much a wine you could sprinkle behind the ears like you do your musk. The flavor is ridden with sweetness, a bit of honey, something remotely apple-esque — simply divine. Simple divine seems quite an oxymoronic phrase as the divine is anything but simple but I’m conviced the idea is clearly communicated. Then again, it is all a matter of taste, and this somehow just matched my palette of preference.
