Every now and then, like just a second ago, I get a bit confused and mix a few words up. The English language just happened to have those pairs of unfortunate words that never really seem quite distinguishable from each other -- at least, that's what it seems like.

Then and than... A single letter a major difference. Then being used to refer to something in relation to a specific moment whereas than is typically used to express comparison.

Efficient or effective... To efficiently get something done has to do with the usage of available resources, effective often refers to the property of achieved a set goal with a higher rate of consistency. It might cost 100x more but miss 3 times as much it's true purpse.
